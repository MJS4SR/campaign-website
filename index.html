Meet Michael J. Stadulis

Candidate • Pennsylvania State Representative • District 121

Service That Started Early

Michael began serving in 1986 at the age of 14 through an observer program at Plains Volunteer Ambulance Association Medic 2, building a lifelong commitment to social responsibility and community service.

  • Graduate of James M. Coughlin High School
  • Luzerne County Community College paramedic curriculum
  • Decades of hands-on public service in District 121

Public Safety & Union Leadership

Michael served with Mercy Hospital Medic 301, Geisinger Medic 303, Spellman Ambulance Medic 8, Hanover Twp. Medic 9, and the Wilkes-Barre Fire Department on Medic 3 and 5 — including service as Chief Paramedic during the last 7 of his 25 years.

As a strong union advocate and champion for employee rights, he served as a Union Steward with LIUNA Laborers Local 1310.

Rooted in the Community

Michael and his wife Renee reside in the city of Wilkes-Barre and raised their son and daughter here. As a family, they are proud to call St. Robert Bellarmine at St. Aloysius their parish home, where they actively practice their Catholic faith and participate in church community fundraising.

Priorities

Focused on what District 121 is living every day

Working Families

Today, most working families must work more than two full-time jobs within a household just to be able to provide for their families and keep up with the rising cost of living.

Public Safety

Public safety resources are strained. District 121 deserves leadership that supports first responders and ensures communities have the resources to stay safe.

Healthcare & Prescriptions

Healthcare and prescription costs continue to rise. Families deserve affordable, reliable access to care.

Utilities & Fees

Utility bills keep increasing with added fees. Families should not be punished by constant increases and new charges.

Infrastructure & Education

Our area’s aging infrastructure demands attention, and school safety and security programs must remain a top priority. We must also advocate for positive education policy upgrades that strengthen opportunities for our students and families.
